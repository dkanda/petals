## 2025-05-15 – Hardened UID Parsing and Server-Side RPC Handling
**Issue:** `parse_uid` used `split()` which limited DHT prefixes to a single level (no dots) and used `assert` for validation. `_push_outputs` had a bug where unpacking failure led to `NameError` in the exception handler due to unbound variables. RPC handlers used `assert` for network-provided metadata validation.
**Risk:** Production servers running with `-O` would skip critical validation, leading to silent state corruption or crashes deeper in the stack. Chained or malformed UIDs could cause unhandled exceptions. Failed pushes would log incorrectly or crash the handler due to `UnboundLocalError`.
**Resolution:** Always use explicit `ValueError`/`TypeError` for validating network-provided data. Use `rsplit(..., 1)` for UID parsing to support multi-level DHT prefixes. Ensure variables referenced in `except` blocks are bound before the potentially failing code, or use safer logging that doesn't depend on them.
